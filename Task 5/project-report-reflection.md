## Reflection: What Surprised Me & What I Will Change

While researching UPI fraud for this project, the most surprising finding was how **little technical skill is required to commit it**. I had assumed cybercrime involved sophisticated hacking. In reality, the most damaging UPI frauds rely entirely on social engineering — specifically, exploiting the confusion between "Send" and "Collect" flows in UPI apps. NPCI data shows that victims themselves approve the transaction, meaning no system is broken into at all. The fraud happens entirely in the victim's mind.

What genuinely shocked me was the recovery rate: only around **22% of UPI fraud cases result in any refund**, even when reported promptly.

**The one habit I will personally change:** I will stop approving any UPI Collect Request that I am not 100% certain I initiated myself — no exceptions, no matter how urgent the caller sounds.
