# 🛡️ Stay Safe Online — Cybercrime Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India | CSA2001 — Task 5

---

## ✅ General Online Safety

- [ ] **1. Use strong, unique passwords for every account**
  Lock your email, social media, and college portal with passwords that are at least 12 characters long and include uppercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse the same password across multiple sites. Use a password manager (like the one built in this project!) to remember them.

- [ ] **2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) everywhere**
  Turn on 2FA for Gmail, Instagram, WhatsApp, and any site that offers it. Even if someone steals your password, they cannot log in without the OTP sent to your phone.

- [ ] **3. Never click links in unsolicited SMS or emails**
  Phishing messages often pretend to be from your bank, NPCI, Amazon, or even your college. Always go directly to the official website by typing the URL yourself — never through a link in a message.

- [ ] **4. Check website URLs before entering any information**
  Fake sites use URLs like `sbi-online-secure.com` instead of `onlinesbi.sbi`. Look for the padlock icon and verify the exact domain name before typing your credentials or card details.

- [ ] **5. Keep your apps and phone OS updated**
  Outdated apps contain known security vulnerabilities that attackers actively exploit. Enable auto-updates for your phone's operating system and all apps, especially your banking and UPI apps.

- [ ] **6. Be cautious on public Wi-Fi — use a VPN**
  Free Wi-Fi at cafés, libraries, and hostels can be monitored by anyone on the same network. Avoid logging into bank accounts or UPI apps on public Wi-Fi. If necessary, use a trusted VPN app.

---

## 💸 Financial & UPI Safety (Required — 2 Tips)

- [ ] **7. ⚠️ NEVER enter your UPI PIN to "receive" money**
  Your PIN is only required when you SEND money, never to receive it. If someone asks you to enter your PIN or scan a QR code to accept a payment (common on OLX, Quikr), it is a fraud. Reject the request immediately.

- [ ] **8. ⚠️ Verify "Collect Requests" before approving**
  In UPI apps like GPay, PhonePe, and Paytm, a Collect Request is a debit from your account — not a credit. Always read the request description carefully. If you don't recognise the sender or amount, decline it. Legitimate buyers never need to send you a collect request.

- [ ] **9. Set a daily UPI transaction limit**
  In your UPI or banking app settings, set a lower daily transaction cap (e.g., ₹5,000) for everyday use. This limits your loss exposure in case of fraud.

- [ ] **10. Never share your OTP, UPI PIN, or CVV with anyone**
  Bank employees, NPCI staff, and UPI apps will NEVER ask for your OTP or PIN over call or message. Sharing these is the single most common way people lose money to fraud.

## 📱 Social Media & Identity Safety

- [ ] **11. Audit your social media privacy settings**
  Set your Instagram, Facebook, and Snapchat profiles to private. Review which apps have access to your account via "Login with Google/Facebook" and revoke access to apps you no longer use.

- [ ] **12. Do not overshare personal details publicly**
  Avoid posting your phone number, home address, college ID, Aadhaar details, or travel plans on public social media. Cybercriminals build profiles from this information to target you.

## 🚨 How to Report Cybercrime in India

If you or someone you know experiences cybercrime, report it **immediately**:

| Channel | Details |
|---|---|
| 🌐 **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) — File an online complaint for any cybercrime including UPI fraud, phishing, cyberbullying, and identity theft |
| 📞 **Helpline Number** | **1930** — Call anytime to report financial/cyber fraud. The faster you call, the higher the chance of freezing the fraudster's account before money is withdrawn |
| 🏠 **Local Cyber Cell** | Visit your nearest police station's Cyber Crime Cell for in-person help |
| 📧 **Email (MHA)** | cybercrime@nic.in |

> **Tip:** When reporting UPI fraud, keep your transaction ID, the fraudster's UPI ID/number, and your bank statement ready. Report within **the first hour** — banks can sometimes freeze the receiving account if acted upon quickly.
